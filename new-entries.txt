Template:ar-nisba/documentation

{{documentation subpage}}
This template is for Arabic {{ar-etym-nisba|lc=1|nocat=1}}, which take the suffix ''-iyy-'' to create an adjective meaning "relating to X" where X is the suffixed noun. See also {{temp|ar-noun-nisba}}, {{temp|ar-adj}}, {{temp|ar-noun}}.

Using this template automatically adds the page to [[Category:Arabic relative adjectives (nisba)]].

Please note that the pronunciation of borrowed nouns, and hence nisba adjectives, often doesn't match their spelling, in that short vowels are often spelled as long vowels to aid in pronunciation. Furthermore, pronunciation of borrowed nouns often includes short and long ''e'' and ''o'', which cannot be indicated using diacritics. Hence, it may not be possible to adequately specify the pronunciation of a borrowed noun using vowel diacritics, and it may also be necessary to explicitly specify a transliteration. All these considerations apply to nisba adjectives as well. Examples is ديموقراطي ''dimuqrāṭiyy'' "democratic", where short ''i'' and ''u'' are spelled with long vowels, and ديالكتيكي ''diyalektīkiyy'' "dialectical", where short ''a'' is spelled long whereas short ''e'' is spelled short (no vowel) and cannot be properly indicated by a diacritic.

==Parameters==
* {{para|1}} indicate diacritic marks EXCEPT for final tanwin "un". This will automatically add diacritics for feminine and plural forms.
* {{para|tr}} transliteration (see [[Wiktionary:About Arabic#Romanization]]). Not usually necessary if diacritics are present in {{para|1}}, as it will be automatically determined from {{para|1}} — but see above about borrowed words. If not present, and diacritics aren't present in {{para|1}}, then the word will be added to the category [[:Category:Arabic terms needing transliteration]].
* {{para|pl}} (optional) overrides the masculine plural for entries such as {{m|ar|عَرَبِيّ|tr=ʿarabiyy}}. Should include diacritics.
* {{para|pl2}} (optional) second masculine plural. If equal to <tt>+</tt>, supply the normal nisba plural (ending in ''-ūn''). Note that it is ''not'' necessary to specify {{para|pl}} when using {{para|pl2}}. Using {{para|pl2}} without {{para|pl}} is the normal way to add a second irregular masculine plural in addition to a first regular masculine plural. (To put the irregular plural first, specify it using {{para|pl}} and then use {{para|pl2|+}}).
* {{para|pltr}} transliteration of overridden masculine plural. Not usually necessary if diacritics are present in {{para|pl}}. Same concerns apply as for {{para|tr}}.
* {{para|pl2tr}} transliteration of second overridden masculine plural. Not usually necessary if diacritics are present in {{para|p2l}}. Same concerns apply as for {{para|tr}}.

With {{para|1}} and optionally {{para|tr}}, feminine and plural forms will be handled automagically.

{{para|tr}} should not be used to show stress, given the somewhat variable nature of Arabic rules regarding which syllable carries stress.

===Relative adjectives (nisba)===
Relative adjectives, also known by the Arabic term [[nisba]], are adjectives formed from another word by adding {{m|ar|ِيّ|tr=-iyy}} (usually pronounced as if written {{m|ar|ِ|ي|tr=-ī}}, also the pronunciation with ''yy'' returns in the feminine {{m|ar||ِيَّة|tr=-iyya}}, the masculine plural {{m|ar||ِيُّون|tr=-iyyūn}}, and the feminine plural {{m|ar||ِيَّات|tr=-iyyāt}}). This construction is quite flexible, and the nisba suffix can be added to any sort of noun, including plurals, as well as in some cases other parts of speech (e.g. {{m|ar|خَارِجِيّ||[[outer]], [[external]]}} from the adjective {{m|ar|خَارِج||[[outer]], [[outside]]}}, {{m|ar|ثَانَوَِيّ||[[secondary]]}} from the adjective {{m|ar|ثَانٍ||[[second]]}}, {{m|ar|أَنَانِيّ||[[egotistical]], [[selfish]]}} from the pronoun {{m|ar|أَنَا|tr=ʾana||[[I]]}}). Nisba adjectives can also be formed directly from foreign words, such as {{m|ar|دِيمُقْرَاطِيّ|tr=dimuqrāṭiyy||[[democratic]]}}, where no such word #{{m|ar||دِيمُقْرَاط|tr=dimuqrāṭ}} exists.

When nisba adjectives are added to a feminine noun in {{m|ar|َة|tr=-a}} or {{m|ar|َاة|tr=-āh}}, this suffix is normally dropped, as in the adjective {{m|ar|مَادِّيّ||[[material]], [[physical]]}}, formed from the noun {{m|ar|مَادَّة||[[matter]], [[material]]}}.  The common suffix {{m|ar|ِيَا|tr=-iyā}} occurring in country names (sometimes written {{m|ar|ِيَة|tr=-iya}}, both forms borrowed from [[-ia]]) is also dropped; for example, from {{m|ar|بِرِيطَانِيَا|tr=biriṭāniyā||[[Britain]]}} is formed {{m|ar|بِرِيطَانِيّ|tr=biriṭāniyy||[[British]]}}. Other suffixes may also be dropped, e.g. from {{m|ar|كِيمِيَاء||[[chemistry]]}} is formed {{m|ar|كِيمِيّ||[[chemical]]}} (also {{m|ar|كِيمِيَائِيّ}} or {{m|ar|كِيمَاوِيّ}}).

In some cases, especially in very short words with only consonants and a short vowel intervening, a linking consonant added, as in {{m|ar|سَنَوِيّ||[[annual]]}} from {{m|ar|سَنَة||[[year]]}}, {{m|ar|نَوَوِيّ||[[nuclear]]}} from {{m|ar|نَوَاة||[[nucleus]]}}, {{m|ar|يَدَوِيّ||[[manual]]}} from {{m|ar|يَد||[[hand]]}}, {{m|ar|دَمَوِيّ||[[blood|blood-]]}} from {{m|ar|دَم||[[blood]]}} (also {{m|ar|دَمِيّ}} without a linking consonant). The particular linking consonant is usually ''w'', but may be a different consonant if that consonant appears in other derived forms of the underlying word; cf. {{m|ar|شَفَهيِّ||[[labial]]}}, also {{m|ar|شَفَوِيّ}}, from {{m|ar|شَفَة||[[lip]]}} (cf. plural {{m|ar|شِفَاه||[[lips]]}}, also {{m|ar|شَفَوَات}}). Note that linking consonants are ''not'' used in cases like {{m|ar|عَادِيّ||[[normal]]}} with a long vowel (from {{m|ar|عَادَة||[[custom]], [[habit]]) or {{m|ar|صِحِّيّ||[[healthy]]}} with a geminate consonant (from {{m|ar|صِحَّة||[[healthy]]}}). Linking consonants may also appear with weak stems ending in {{m|ar||ٍ|tr=-in}} or {{m|ar||ًا|tr=-an}}, e.g. {{m|ar|ثَانَوَِيّ||[[secondary]]}} from {{m|ar|ثَانٍ||[[second]]}}. Examples with a linking {{m|ar||َان|tr=-ān-}} are {{m|ar|أَنَانِيّ||[[egotistical]], [[selfish]]}} from {{m|ar|أَنَا|tr=ʾana||[[I]]}} and {{m|ar|عَلْمَانِيّ||[[secular]], [[worldly]]}} from {{m|ar|عَالَم||[[world]]}} (note also the distortion of the stem in the latter case).

===Relative nouns (nisba)===
Relative ([[nisba]]) adjectives can be freely made into nouns. For example, from the adjective {{m|ar|بِرِيطَانِيّ|tr=biriṭāniyy||[[British]]}} is derived the noun {{m|ar|بِرِيطَانِيّ|tr=biriṭāniyy||[[British]] man, [[Briton]]}} and from the adjective {{m|ar|دِيمُقْرَاطِيّ|tr=dimuqrāṭiyy||[[democratic]]}} is formed the noun {{m|ar|دِيمُقْرَاطِيّ|tr=dimuqrāṭiyy||[[democrat]]}}. These nouns form feminines and plurals the same way as the corresponding adjectives.

In addition, the feminine nisba noun frequently has the meaning of an abstract noun, e.g. {{m|ar|دِيمُقْرَاطِيَّة|tr=dimuqrāṭiyya||[[democracy]]}} from {{m|ar|دِيمُقْرَاطِيّ|tr=dimuqrāṭiyy||[[democratic]]}} or {{m|ar|ذِهْنِيَّة||[[mentality]]}} from {{m|ar|ذِهْنيِّ||[[mental]]}} (in turn formed from {{m|ar|ذِهْن||[[mind]]}}). In some cases, it is best to view these nouns as being formed directly using a suffix {{m|ar|ِيَّة|tr=-iyya}}, especially since the corresponding word in {{m|ar|ِيّ|tr=-iyy}} may not exist. Examples are {{m|ar|كَمِّيَة||[[quantity]]}} formed from {{m|ar|كَمّ||[[quantity]]}}, {{m|ar|أَهَمِّيَة||[[importance]]}} formed from {{m|ar|أَهَمّ||more [[important]], very [[important]]}} and {{m|ar|هُوِيَّة||[[identity]]}} formed from {{m|ar|هُوَ||[[he]]}} (note that the last two examples are formed from parts of speech other than nouns).

Format of the following:
  ELATIVEDESC = TEXT ELATIVE DECL from ARPOSITIVE from ROOT
  ELATIVE = (Arabic)
  ARPOSITIVE = "(Arabic)" or "(Arabic) and (Arabic) ..."
  ROOT = (Arabic) or "(Arabic) and (Arabic) ..."
  DECL = declensional forms, ignored currently
  TEXT = one or more DEFN, separated by slashes
  DEFN = one or more SYNONYM, separated by commas, or text in parens
  SYNONYM = "POSITIVE" or "POSITIVE [COMPARATIVE]" or "POSITIVE [COMPARATIVE; SUPERLATIVE]" or "POSITIVE []"

Each DEFN takes up one line. Text in parens is inserted exactly. For SYNONYMs, comparative and superlative are generated from the positive form as follows:
1. SYNONYM = POSITIVE: comparative is "more POSITIVE", superlative is "most POSITIVE"
2. SYNONYM = POSITIVE [COMPARATIVE]: comparative is "COMPARATIVE", superlative is formed from comparative using s/er$/est$/
3. SYNONYM = POSITIVE [COMPARATIVE; SUPERLATIVE]: comparative and superlative are as given
4. SYNONYM = POSITIVE []: no comparative or superlative; intended for the case where the elative has a special meaning not derivable from the positive
The defn line groups all comparative synonyms together, separated by commas, then a semicolon, then all superlative synonyms, separated by commas. In case #4 above where there is no comparative/superlative, the positive form is inserted where the comparative would normally go. All words except "more" and "most" and text in parens are auto-bracketed e.g. "[[prettier]]" or "more [[beautiful]]". If there are multiple words in a POSITIVE entry, each is bracketed individually, e.g. "more [[highly]] [[esteemed]]".
